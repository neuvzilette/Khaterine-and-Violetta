Our Story

Tzu Chi Indonesia (慈济), Non-Profit Organization

Tzu Chi Indonesia is a humanitarian organization committed to promoting compassion and relief efforts across the country, focusing on areas such as healthcare, education, environmental protection, and community development, while inspiring people to cultivate kindness and selflessness.

Master Cheng Yen

Founder

Master Cheng Yen, born in 1937, is the founder of the Buddhist Tzu Chi Foundation, a global humanitarian organization known for its dedication to compassion, charity, and relief efforts.

Her vision of “relieving suffering and spreading kindness” continues to inspire millions, fostering a spirit of love, humility, and respect among diverse communities.

Bencana Palu

Gempa bumi dan tsunami yang terjadi di Kota Palu dan Donggala Sulawesi Tengah pada Jumat, 28 September 2018. Tercatat 1000 jiwa lebih meninggal dunia dalam bencana ini. Bantuan pun segera mengalir ke wilayah Kota Palu yang mengalami dampak paling parah. Pandemi COVID-19

Pandemi mencapai puncaknya di Indonesia pada Juli 2021, tetapi paruh pertama Agustus masih mencatat rata-rata harian 20.000 hingga 30.000 infeksi baru. Sejak Februari 2021, Tzu Chi Indonesia telah menawarkan layanan inokulasi untuk membantu mempercepat program vaksinasi di negara ini, dan pusat pandemi dibuka pada 14 Juni 2021, untuk membantu perawatan dan isolasi pasien yang terinfeksi. Lima ribu konsentrator oksigen dan beras untuk tujuh juta rumah tangga juga didistribusikan untuk membantu masyarakat melewati krisis.

COVID-19 Donation For Rural Areas

For the past year, the Buddhist Tzu Chi Foundation (Tzu Chi) has been making a concerted effort to send food and medical supplies to those countries hit hardest by the COVID-19 pandemic. As of June 20, 2021, Tzu Chi’s footprints of humanitarian aid have now reached 125 countries and regions. Since the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic, Tzu Chi has sent pandemic relief to 93 countries and regions in the world, with a total of 34,096,312 pieces of medical supplies.
